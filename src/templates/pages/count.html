Flesch Reading Ease Model

Learn more

The Flesch Reading Ease Formula is a simple approach to assess the grade-level of the reader. It’s also one of the few accurate measures around that we can rely on without too much scrutiny. This formula is best used on school text. It has since become a standard readability formula used by many US Government Agencies, including the US Department of Defense. However, primarily, we use the formula to assess the difficulty of a reading passage written in English.

Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

Learn more

The Flesch Kincaid Grade Level is a widely used readability formula which assesses the approximate reading grade level of a text. It was developed by the US Navy who worked with the Flesch Reading Ease. If a text has a Flesch Kincaid level of 8, this means the reader needs a grade 8 level of reading or above to understand it. Even if they’re an advanced reader, it means the content is less time-consuming to read.

SMOG (FOG) Index

Learn more

The SMOG index is an acronym for Simple Measure of Gobbledygook. This readability model was developed by G. Harry McLaughlin, and was created as an improvement of other readability formulas. The FOG index, as it is also known as, estimates the years of education a person would need to understand a piece of writing, and it was created.

Coleman-Liau

The Coleman–Liau Readability Formula (also known as The Coleman–Liau Index) is a readability assessment model designed by linguists Meri Coleman and T. L. Liau to approximate the usability of a text. this model is one of the standards used by the U.S. Office of Education to guage thereadability of all textbooks for the public school system.
